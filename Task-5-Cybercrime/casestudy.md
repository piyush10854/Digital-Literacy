#Cybercrime Awareness Case Study
#Cybercrime Type: UPI / Online Payment Fraud (Real Incident)

UPI fraud is one of the fastest-growing cybercrimes in India. A real incident reported in Delhi (2025) involved two fraudsters who stole ₹20 lakh using stolen smartphones and banking apps. The case came to light when a Delhi resident lost his phone and later received notifications of unauthorized transactions from his bank account.

According to police, the scammers purchased lost or stolen mobile phones, reset them, and then accessed banking applications already linked to those devices. They used Aadhaar-linked authentication and reset PINs to transfer money into fake accounts. The fraudsters were linked to over 100 similar cases across the city before being arrested.

How the Fraud Happened (Step-by-Step)
Victim lost their smartphone
Scammers obtained the device and reset it
Banking apps were accessed using stored information
PINs were reset using authentication methods
Money was transferred to fake accounts
Victim noticed unauthorized transactions later

Students, working professionals, and elderly individuals are often targeted, especially those who use mobile banking regularly.

The consequences included financial loss, stress, and time spent filing complaints with banks and police. Since UPI transfers are instant, recovering money becomes difficult.

This case highlights the importance of locking phones, enabling SIM locks, avoiding storing sensitive information, and immediately reporting lost devices. Digital awareness and quick action can help prevent such cybercrimes.
